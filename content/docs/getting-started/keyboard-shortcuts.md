## The two that matter

Ampliterm's keyboard story centers on two global shortcuts (`⌘` on macOS, `Ctrl` on Windows/Linux):

| Shortcut | Action |
|---|---|
| `⌘K` | Open the **command palette** — search and go anywhere |
| `⌘J` | Toggle **Cortex**, the AI assistant panel |

## The command palette

`⌘K` is the fastest way to move through the terminal. Start typing to search **tickers by symbol or company name**, or filter the built-in destinations and actions:

**Navigate** — Markets, Screener, Journal, Trades, Predictions, Simulations, Cortex History, Settings.

**Act** — New Journal Entry, Price Prediction (opens the quick-log modal right where you are), New Simulation.

Inside the palette: `↑` `↓` to move, `↵` to select, `Esc` to close.

## In editors and modals

| Shortcut | Where | Action |
|---|---|---|
| `⌘↵` | Cortex composer | Send the message |
| `⌘↵` | Add Ticker / Price Prediction modals | Submit |
| `Esc` | Any modal or the palette | Close |

That's the honest, complete list — Ampliterm keeps chrome minimal and puts its speed into the palette rather than a large chord vocabulary. New shortcuts will land here as they ship.
