## Your research inbox

Trading research is scattered by nature — an article here, a PDF there, an earnings call you half-remember. **Intel** is the capture layer: everything you read, hear, or upload lands in one searchable feed, tied to the tickers it's about, with AI doing the first-pass reading.

Where the [Journal](/docs/user-guide/journal) holds what *you* think, Intel holds what you've *gathered* — the inputs to the thinking.

## Four kinds of items

- **Clips** — captured web content. Source metadata comes along for the ride: the original URL, the source site, and a preview image.
- **Notes** — quick freeform capture when the insight is yours but doesn't warrant a journal entry.
- **Files** — uploads: PDFs, reports, whatever documents your research produces.
- **Transcripts** — earnings calls, conference calls, and podcasts, typed by event so a quarter's earnings narrative is easy to reassemble later.

Items are processed in the background when they arrive (you'll see pending → ready on fresh captures), extracting metadata and preparing content for analysis.

## Capture from anywhere

Inside the app, the Intel page has capture modals for clips, notes, and uploads — plus a URL fetcher that pulls a page's metadata for you. But the natural habitat of research is the browser, so Ampliterm pairs with a **browser extension**: authenticate it with an API token from **Settings → API Tokens**, and clipping an article into your instance happens where you're reading it, not after a copy-paste round trip.

## AI does the first pass

Run analysis on any item (it's queued in the background) and it gains an **AI summary**, **key bullet points**, and a **sentiment read** — bullish, bearish, or neutral. That turns a 40-page PDF or an hour-long call transcript into something you can triage in the feed, expanding only what deserves your full attention. The usual posture applies: AI prioritizes your reading; it doesn't make your decisions.

## Organized around tickers

Intel items link to tickers — one item can inform several symbols, and each ticker accumulates its research trail. With the filter bar and sidebar you can slice the feed by type, status, or ticker, and set your own **confidence** on items so the feed reflects how much weight you actually give each source.
