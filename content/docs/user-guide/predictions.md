## Calibration, not forecasting

A prediction in Ampliterm is a small, honest bet with yourself: *this ticker will be at this price by this date.* Write it down, and when the date arrives, Ampliterm scores it against what actually happened — accurate or not, on the record, forever.

The point isn't to become an oracle. It's **calibration**: after twenty scored predictions you know something concrete about your own judgment — whether your instincts on a name deserve trust, whether you're systematically optimistic, whether your three-month calls beat your one-week ones. Most traders never learn this because nobody's keeping score. Ampliterm keeps score.

## Making a prediction

Log one from wherever the thought strikes: the **Predictions** page, a [ticker page](/docs/user-guide/markets) (predictions live alongside the chart they're about), or the **⌘K command palette** → *Price Prediction* — a ticker, a price, a date, done.

## How scoring works

Once a prediction's date passes, it's resolved against that day's **closing price**. "Accurate" means within your tolerance — a percentage deviation you set yourself in **Settings → Profile**. A ±2% tolerance and a ±10% tolerance are both legitimate; what matters is that you picked it *before* the scoring, not after.

Results are recorded on the prediction — predicted price, actual price, verdict — and you can get an email when one resolves (a notification preference, on by default).

## Reading your record

The Predictions page is your ledger of calls: open predictions waiting on their date, and resolved ones with their verdicts. Each ticker page shows your history on that specific name — which is often the more humbling view. Pair it with the [Journal](/docs/user-guide/journal): the prediction records *what* you called, the journal entry records *why*, and the two together are how judgment actually improves.
