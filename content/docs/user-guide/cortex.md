## An analyst in the terminal

Cortex is Ampliterm's conversational AI assistant — open it from the topbar or with **⌘J** on any page. It's built to be a conservative research analyst: it runs real indicator math, reads your own trading data, critiques your strategy logic, and streams its reasoning back as a conversation. What it never does is tell you what to buy. Every response renders under an amber **ANALYSIS · NOT ADVICE** note, and that posture is enforced by the application, not left to the model's discretion.

## Enabling Cortex

Cortex uses your own AI provider account. The instance owner configures it under **Super Admin → Integrations → AI**: pick a provider (OpenAI or Anthropic), a model, and paste an API key. Like your broker keys, it's stored encrypted on your own server.

Until AI is configured, Cortex simply isn't there — the button and panel hide themselves. Once configured, usage is billed by your provider under your key, so you control the spend. (Messages are also rate-limited to 20 per minute per user as a guardrail.)

## What Cortex can actually do

Cortex isn't a chatbot with opinions — it wields a library of deterministic tools, and the numbers come from the same math the engine trades on:

- **Indicator & signal evaluations** — ATR volatility, Bollinger Bands, EMA crossover, MACD, RSI, relative volume, VWAP, and engulfing-candle analysis. When you ask "how does the RSI look on this chart?", Cortex calls the evaluation tool with the ticker you're viewing; the tool computes the actual values and Cortex interprets them. It fetches candles itself, so you never paste data.
- **Your own trading data** — three tools that read *your* records and nobody else's: your open trades, your strategy deployments, and a search over your journal entries. Ask "how are my open positions doing against what I wrote when I opened them?" and it can genuinely answer.
- **What you're looking at** — on a ticker page, Cortex automatically knows the symbol and timeframe (you'll see context chips in the composer), and it can capture the chart you're viewing as an image to reason about visually. The empty state suggests prompts relevant to the page you're on.

## Strategy-draft critique

The standout workflow: while you're building a strategy — even before saving it — Cortex can see the **in-progress draft** exactly as it stands on your screen. Open the panel mid-build and ask "what's the weakness in this entry logic?" or "what would you add as a bail-out guard?" — it reads your trigger, confirmation tree, exits, guards, risk rules, and sizing, and critiques them in the builder's own vocabulary, suggesting only conditions that actually exist in the [catalog](/docs/user-guide/condition-catalog).

It's explicitly forbidden from making profitability claims about your draft — it critiques structure and logic, not future returns. For measuring results, that's what [backtests](/docs/user-guide/simulations) are for.

## Conversations

Every thread persists. Close the panel mid-conversation and reopen it later, switch between threads, or browse the full history on the **Cortex History** page (reach it through the ⌘K command palette, or at `/cortex`) — conversations are titled automatically and can be deleted permanently. Threads are private to your account.

## The guardrails, spelled out

Worth being explicit about, since an AI assistant inside a trading tool deserves scrutiny:

- **No advice, structurally.** The disclaimer under every message is rendered by the app itself — never generated by (or dependent on) the model. Cortex's instructions mandate hedged, factual analysis and prohibit recommendations and price predictions.
- **Your data stays yours.** The own-data tools are scoped server-side to your account; the model can't request anyone else's trades or journal.
- **Deterministic core.** Indicator values come from the tool layer's math — the same calculations the engine uses — not from the model's imagination. The model's job is interpretation.
- **Optional everywhere.** Cortex is a research aid layered on top of Ampliterm. Nothing in the strategy engine depends on it — with the sole exception of the [AI confirmation gate](/docs/user-guide/strategy-builder#ai-confirmation-gate), which you opt into per strategy and which can only ever veto entries, not create them.
